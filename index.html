Puzzle 1 — Multiplying by 11 (the quick pattern)

Recap: Use a pattern to multiply numbers by 11 quickly.

Two-digit numbers

Let the number be ab (digits a then b). Then 11 × ab becomes a (a+b) b, with a carry if a+b ≥ 10.

  • 34 × 11 → 3 (3+4) 4 → 374.
  • 59 × 11 → 5 (5+9) 9 → carry: 5+1 | 4 | 9 → 649.

Why it works: 11×(10a+b)=110a+11b=100a + (a+b)·10 + b.

Three-digit numbers

For digits a b c, write: a | (a+b) | (b+c) | c, managing carries.

  • 314 × 11 → 3 | 3+1=4 | 1+4=5 | 4 → 3454.
  • 907 × 11 → 9 | 9+0=9 | 0+7=7 | 7 → 9977.
Puzzle 2 — Why every even-digit palindrome is divisible by 11

Recap: Prove that any palindrome with an even number of digits is a multiple of 11.

Proof (using the 11 divisibility test)

The rule: a number is divisible by 11 iff the alternating sum of its digits is a multiple of 11.

For a 2m-digit palindrome a₁ a₂ … aₘ aₘ … a₂ a₁, the alternating sum is (a₁ − a₂ + a₃ − … + aₘ) − (aₘ − … + a₃ − a₂ + a₁), which cancels to 0. Hence it’s divisible by 11.

Examples: 1221 = 11 × 111, 7447 = 11 × 677, 1331 = 11 × 121.

Puzzle 3 — How many diagonals does a regular hendecagon have?

Recap: A hendecagon has 11 sides. How many diagonals does it contain?

Solution

In an n-gon the number of diagonals is C(n,2) − n = n(n−1)/2 − n = n(n−3)/2. For n = 11, that’s 11×8/2 = 44 diagonals.

Optional extension: regions formed by all diagonals

When no three diagonals meet at an interior point, the number of regions is C(n,4) + C(n,2) + 1. For n=11, that gives 330 + 55 + 1 = 386.
